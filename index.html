Flight routes — Wellington to Armenia / Salento
There are no direct flights from New Zealand to Colombia — at least one connection is required. All prices shown are return economy per person. Booking 6 or more months in advance typically yields the best fares. Most routes involve a short Bogotá transit (2–3 hours) to connect domestically to Armenia; this is not an overnight stay. Avianca and LATAM both run frequent 45-minute Bogotá–Armenia (AXM) connections.
Route A — Via Santiago, ChileRecommended for travellers with health considerations
LATAM Airlines operates a daily Auckland–Santiago service, with onward connections to Pereira (PEI) — Colombia's "City of Eternal Spring" — arriving without transiting Bogotá. A 1–2 night rest stop in Santiago is strongly recommended to break the journey and allow the body to recover before arriving at altitude.

Note on Bogotá transit
Except for Route A (via Santiago → Pereira), most routes pass through Bogotá for a short domestic connection to Armenia — typically 2–3 hours, not an overnight stay. Bogotá sits at 2,600m, significantly higher than Armenia (1,483m). Travellers with cardiovascular conditions should transit swiftly, stay well hydrated, and avoid exertion in the airport.

Climate by month
Armenia is warmer (23–28°C days) at 1,483m. Salento is cooler (20–23°C days) at ~1,950m — typically 4–6°C lower, with cool nights requiring a light layer. Both follow Colombia's bimodal rain pattern: drier and sunnier in January–February and July–August, wetter in April–May and October–November.

Altitude sickness — what to know
Altitude sickness (acute mountain sickness, or AMS) occurs when the body doesn't have enough time to adjust to lower oxygen levels at elevation. It can affect anyone regardless of age or fitness — previous experience at altitude offers no guarantee of immunity. Symptoms typically appear within 6–24 hours of arrival and resolve within 1–3 days with rest. For people with pre-existing heart or lung conditions, the effects can be more pronounced, and a doctor should be consulted well before travel.
Mild · most common
Common symptoms
  • Headache (most frequent)
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Slight breathlessness
  • Dizziness or light-headedness
  • Disrupted or poor sleep
  • Loss of appetite
Moderate · rest & monitor
Signs to watch for
  • Persistent or worsening headache
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Difficulty walking steadily
  • Shortness of breath at rest
  • Unusual or extreme fatigue
  • Confusion or irritability
Severe · descend immediately
Emergency signs
  • Chest tightness or pain
  • Inability to walk or stand
  • Severe breathlessness at rest
  • Coughing up frothy / pink fluid
  • Confusion or unconsciousness
  • Emergency: call 123 in Colombia

Prevention tips: Arrive gradually — Route A via Pereira gives the gentlest possible entry into Colombia. Plan 1–2 rest days on arrival before any strenuous activity. Drink 2–3 litres of water daily. Avoid alcohol and heavy meals in the first 24–48 hours. Ibuprofen or paracetamol can ease mild headaches. Acetazolamide (Diamox) is a prescription medication that aids acclimatisation — worth discussing with a GP before departure, particularly for anyone with a cardiovascular or respiratory condition.
For travellers with heart or cardiovascular conditions
Altitude reduces the oxygen available with each heartbeat, placing additional strain on the cardiovascular system. Armenia (1,483m) is considerably gentler than Bogotá (2,600m) and makes a far better base. Arriving via Pereira (1,400m) on Route A gives the easiest possible acclimatisation. A cardiologist or GP should be consulted at least 6–8 weeks before departure to review medications and provide personalised guidance for travelling at moderate altitude.
Health & medical care
In Armenia (city)
  • Hospital Departamental San Juan de Dios (public)
  • Clínica Versalles — private, recommended for visitors
  • English-speaking doctors at private clinics
  • Pharmacies (droguerías) citywide, very affordable

Near Salento
  • Salento itself has basic first-aid only — a small village
  • Armenia: full hospital ~25 km, ~30 min by taxi
  • Pereira: excellent hospitals ~45 km away
  • Taxis and private drivers available around the clock
  • Emergency number in Colombia: 123
Colombia's healthcare
  • WHO ranked 22nd globally — highest in South America
  • Private consultations: ~NZD 35–85 out of pocket
  • JCI-accredited hospitals in Bogotá, Medellín & Cali
  • Strong cardiology units in major cities
  • Most medications available; carry a generic-name list
Travel insurance — essentials
  • NZ SafeTravel recommends comprehensive cover for Colombia
  • Policy must explicitly cover any pre-existing conditions
  • Medical evacuation by air should be included
  • See a doctor 6–8 weeks before travel for a health review
  • Carry medications in original packaging with a doctor's letter

Colombian hospitality
  • Colombians are among the most welcoming people in South America
  • Salento and Armenia are well-established tourist destinations
  • A few words of Spanish go a long way — gracias, por favor, cuánto cuesta
  • Tipping ~10% in restaurants is appreciated but not mandatory
  • Cash (pesos) useful for markets, Willy Jeeps and small purchases
Accommodation — all prices in NZD per night
Armenia
4-star city hotelsNZD 100–220 / night
Mocawa Plaza and Hotel Armenia offer pools, restaurants, room service and reliable amenities. English-speaking staff at most. Breakfast typically included. The most comfortable and convenient base for guests wanting ease of access to everything.
Haciendas & eco-lodgesNZD 135–300 / night
Hacienda Bambusa and Hacienda Buenavista sit amid coffee farms with spectacular valley views, private pools, and full meal service. Ideal for guests wanting a quieter, more immersive experience in the Colombian countryside.
Mid-range guesthousesNZD 50–100 / night
A wide range of solid 3-star options near Plaza Bolívar, restaurants and pharmacies. A practical choice for guests managing their budget without sacrificing comfort or location.
Getting around ArmeniaNZD 10–35 per trip
Uber and local taxis are widely available and inexpensive. Approximately 30 minutes to Salento and 15 minutes to the airport. A private driver for the day costs around NZD 85–135.
Salento
Finca (coffee farm) staysNZD 85–200 / night
Reserva Guadalajara in the Cocora Valley is a favourite — fresh farm breakfasts, mountain views, and staff who arrange activities and transport. Peaceful and genuinely special, well suited to guests wanting comfort without the bustle of town.
Boutique hotels in townNZD 65–170 / night
Hotel Salento Plaza and Hotel Salento Real are centrally located, well-reviewed, and colourfully designed. Walking distance to restaurants and the main square, with Willy Jeep rides to Cocora Valley departing right from the plaza.
